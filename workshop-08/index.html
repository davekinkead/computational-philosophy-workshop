Information Cascades

An information cascade is a situation of rational choice in which individuals base their decisions on the observations of others, regardless of their private belief.

In this simulation, agents are randomly assigned a belief about some proposition from p(0.0) - in red - to p(1.0) - in blue. In each tick, the agents express their belief as either true p>(0.5) or false p<(0.5) and then update their own belief based on the expressions of their neighbours. Everyone is assumed to have equal prior chance of holding the correct belief.

What we see is that information cascades rapidly and polarise beliefs because when all your neighbours hold an opposing belief, the rational thing to do is adjust yours (assumng everyone is equi-likely to be correct.)
